Interpersonal protest recruiting

My doctoral research focuses on protest participation. More specifically, I examine the micro-level predictors and outcomes of interpersonal protest recruiting (aka. "asking others" to protest). I mainly use quantitative panel survey data to analyze my research questions. Thematically, I have covered labor union demonstrations, solidarity movements, and the climate movement.

Using a predominantly quantitative methodological approach, I have gained real experience with data handling and (advanced) analysis (e.g. longitudinal egocentric network data). I consider myself an expert with Qualtrics and Stata, and an ongoing student of R and Python. I currently assist academic professionals with various aspects of survey programming, dataset merging and preparation, as well as designing data visualizations.

Together with valued coauthors and excellent promotors, I have published two high-impact international descriptive reports on Fridays For Future and one peer-reviewed essay on contemporary climate activism (combined 860+ citations), four other peer-reviewed articles in top-ranking sociology journals (on the topic of interpersonal protest recruiting), and three public reports (on Belgian protests). Two other peer-reviewed publications and one encyclopedia entry are currently forthcoming.
